SE595 - Empirical Software Engineering

Course Description

Empirical Software Engineering is used to understand how software artifacts are used in model building, experimentation and learning. Software Engineering is no different, yet remarkably little work has been done in this area. In this course we discuss the scientific process and how we use it in the software engineering field. This course will explore the role of empiricism in software engineering research. It will prepare students for advanced research by examining how to plan, conduct and report on empirical investigations. The course will cover techniques applicable to each step of a research project, including formulating research questions, theory building, data analysis (using both qualitative and quantitative methods), building evidence, and assessing validity. The will prepare students to conduct research that requires some form of empirical validation.

Resources
